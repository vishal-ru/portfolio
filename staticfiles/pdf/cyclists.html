Cyclists Bike Usage Analytics

Objective: Analyze How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

Dataset: June 2025 Dataset

In this project, we explore how annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bike-share services differently. Using one month of ride data from Chicago, we perform detailed data cleaning, transformation, and visualization. The analysis focuses on ride duration, trip timing, and station popularity. Insights from this study help inform marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into annual members.

Business Question: How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

Our analysis reveals clear behavioral differences:

  • Casual Riders mostly begin their rides from scenic, recreational locations such as Streeter Dr & Grand Ave, Millennium Park, and Theater on the Lake. These are popular tourist spots, indicating that casual riders typically use bikes for leisure and exploration, especially on weekends and holidays.

  • Annual Members, on the other hand, frequently start rides at stations near office areas, residential neighborhoods, and transit hubs like Kingsbury St, Clinton St, and Wells St. This suggests that members rely on bikes for daily commuting, errands, or consistent transportation needs.

  • Ride Duration & Frequency : Casual riders usually take longer but less frequent rides, while members take shorter, more regular rides reinforcing the recreational vs. utilitarian usage pattern.

Overall, casual riders ride for fun, and members ride with purpose. These insights are vital for shaping marketing strategies, pricing models, and expansion plans.
